## PAGE 1: HOME

### Bio / About

Eric So is the **Sloan Distinguished Professor of Global Economics and Behavioral Science** at MIT. His work combines economics and behavioral science to study the interactions between human nature, incentive systems, and technology. He is a tenured full professor and a member of both the **Global Economics and Management** group and the **Behavioral and Policy Sciences** area at the MIT Sloan School of Management.

### AI & Leadership Roles

- **Faculty Co-Director**, AI Executive Academy (https://executive.mit.edu/course/ai-executive-academy/a05U1000002o7uoIAA.html)
- **Lead Faculty**, MIT Sloan Generative AI Hub for Teaching and Learning (https://mitsloanedtech.mit.edu/ai/)
- **Faculty Chair**, MIT Sloan PhD Program

### Research Focus Areas

Current research spans: artificial intelligence (AI), behavioral economics, human-computer interactions, and regulatory policy. His work seeks to bridge academic research and real-world application.

### Education

- **PhD** — Stanford University, Graduate School of Business
- **Master's in Economics** — Cornell University
- Joined MIT in 2012

### Book

*Alphanomics: The Informational Underpinnings of Market Efficiency*

### Teaching

Currently teaches courses on AI applications in business and investment decisions.

## PAGE 5: DATA LIBRARY

### Dataset 1: Implied Cost of Capital and Expected Return Data
- **Data**: https://leesowang2021.github.io/data/
- **Paper**: "Evaluating Firm-Level Expected-Return Proxies" — Lee, Wang, So — *Review of Financial Studies*
- **Range**: Updated somewhat regularly
- **Fields**: See link for details

### Dataset 2: Expectations Management Incentive (EMI) Data
- **Data**: https://www.dropbox.com/s/kc7u4gowjkggdgk/EMI_RFS.csv?dl=0
- **Paper**: "Expectations Management and Stock Returns" — Johnson, Kim, So — *Review of Financial Studies*
- **Range**: 1985–2015
- **Type**: Quarterly earnings announcement by firm
- **Fields**: Permno, Expected_ann_date, EMI (proxy for expectations management incentives)

### Dataset 3: Daily Information Asymmetry Proxy
- **Data**: https://www.dropbox.com/s/4hlqlws7dqs8c5o/miadata1996to2018.sas7bdat?dl=0
- **Paper**: "A Simple Multimarket Measure of Information Asymmetry" — Johnson, So — *Management Science*
- **Range**: 2006–2018
- **Type**: Firm-by-day
- **Fields**: Permno, Date, MIA (daily proxy for the fraction of traders with an information advantage)

### Dataset 4: Annual Earnings Forecasts from Regression Model
- **Data**: https://www.dropbox.com/preview/Documents/Published%20Papers/OAF/Data/ericso_epsforecasts.sas7bdat?share=1
- **Paper**: "A New Approach to Predicting Analyst Forecast Errors" — So — *Journal of Financial Economics* 108(3), Jun 2013
- **Range**: 1978–2016
- **Type**: Firm-by-year
- **Fields**: GVKEY, CUSIP, FYEAR (fiscal year), FYR (month of fiscal year end), ForecastDate, WF1/2/3 (1-, 2-, 3-year ahead EPS forecasts), Shares (shares outstanding)
